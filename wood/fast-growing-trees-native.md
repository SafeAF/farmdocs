# Fast-Growing Native Trees

## Species Overview

- Poplar
- Cottonwood
- Quaking aspen
- Bur oak
- Hackberry
- Kentucky coffeetree
- Honey locust (thornless if desired)
- Serviceberry
- Chokecherry

## Where to Plant

Think 30 years ahead.

### Don't plant near:

- Septic systems
- Water lines
- Foundations
- Your barns
- Irrigation pipelines

**Note:** Poplar roots are aggressive and will seek water.

## Planting Instructions

1. Dig as much root ball as practical
2. Water thoroughly before digging
3. Plant at the same depth
4. Water deeply after planting
5. Mulch around it (but keep mulch off the trunk)

## Cloning Methods

### 1. Hardwood Cuttings (Recommended)

This is almost ridiculously easy.

**In winter:**

1. Cut pencil-thick branches, 8–12 inches long
2. Ensure several buds on each cutting
3. Make a straight cut on the top and an angled cut on the bottom to remember orientation
4. Stick the bottom half into moist soil

Many poplars root with 70–100% success. You can even root them in water first if you want to watch the roots develop.

### 2. Root Cuttings

1. Dig up one lateral root during dormancy
2. Cut it into 3–6 inch sections
3. Plant horizontally just below the soil surface

They'll often sprout entirely new trees.

3. Root suckers

Once the tree matures you'll probably get volunteers several feet away.

Those can simply be dug up and replanted.
